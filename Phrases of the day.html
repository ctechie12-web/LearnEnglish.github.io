Bonus lesson: Phrasal verbs

A quick guide before you go!

A phrasal verb is a verb combined with a preposition or adverb (called a "particle") that creates a brand-new meaning — often very different from the original verb. For example, look means to see, but look after means to take care of someone!

🔤 Types of phrasal verbs
🔗
Intransitive — no object needed

Example: The plane took off smoothly.

🎯
Transitive separable — object can go in the middle or end

Example: She switched off the light / switched the light off.

🔒
Transitive inseparable — object always comes after

Example: They looked after the puppy. (not "looked the puppy after")

⭐ Common phrasal verbs to remember
Wake up
to stop sleeping
"I woke up early today."
Look after
to take care of
"She looks after her brother."
Give up
to stop trying
"Never give up on your dreams."
Put off
to postpone
"They put off the match."
Carry on
to continue
"Please carry on with your work."
Turn down
to refuse
"He turned down the offer."
💡 Why phrasal verbs matter

Just like "wee hours of the dawn" adds color to plain sentences, phrasal verbs make your English sound natural and fluent. Native speakers use them constantly in everyday conversation — try spotting one every day in what you read!
